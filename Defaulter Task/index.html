Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani)

⏱ Prep: 30 mins | Cook: 40 mins 🔪 Medium

Tender chicken pieces simmered down in an incredibly rich, creamy, and heavily spiced tomato-butter gravy.

Ingredients

  • 500g Boneless chicken thighs
  • 1 cup thick yogurt (for marinade)
  • 2 tbsp Kashmiri chili powder
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 4 large tomatoes, pureed
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp dried fenugreek leaves (Kasuri Methi)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken: Mix the yogurt, half the chili powder, half the ginger-garlic paste, and salt. Coat the chicken completely and marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Cook the chicken: Heat a pan with oil and sear the marinated chicken pieces until slightly charred and cooked through. Remove from pan.
  3. Make the sauce base: In the same pan, melt the butter. Add the remaining ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil begins to separate.
  4. Spice it up: Stir in the remaining chili powder and salt. Cook for another 5 minutes. Add a splash of water if the sauce is too thick.
  5. Finish the dish: Return the chicken to the pan. Reduce the heat and stir in the heavy cream, garam masala, and crushed dried fenugreek leaves. Simmer gently for 5 minutes. Serve with hot naan!

Palak Paneer

⏱ Prep: 15 mins | Cook: 25 mins 🔪 Medium

A classic vegetarian dish consisting of soft paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cubes in a smooth, mildly spiced spinach sauce.

Ingredients

  • 250g Paneer, cubed
  • 500g Fresh spinach leaves
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • ¼ cup heavy cream

Instructions

  1. Blanch the spinach: Boil a pot of water. Add spinach leaves for 2 minutes, then immediately transfer them into ice-cold water to retain their bright green color.
  2. Blend: Blend the blanched spinach along with the green chilies into a smooth puree. Set aside.
  3. Aromatics: Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add the chopped onion and sauté until golden brown. Add the ginger-garlic paste and cook for an additional minute.
  4. Tomato base: Add the pureed tomatoes, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until the raw smell of tomatoes goes away and the oil begins to separate.
  5. Combine: Pour in the spinach puree and mix well. Let it simmer gently for 5 minutes. Finally, add the paneer cubes and heavy cream. Stir carefully to avoid breaking the paneer. Serve hot with roti or jeera rice.

Chicken Biryani

⏱ Prep: 45 mins | Cook: 1 hr 🔪 Hard

A world-renowned royal tier dish featuring highly aromatic basmati rice layered with deeply spiced, tender chicken.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain Basmati rice, soaked
  • 500g Chicken, bone-in pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 3 large onions, thinly sliced and fried (Birista)
  • 2 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 3 tbsp biryani masala powder
  • Whole spices (cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, bay leaves)
  • Pinch of saffron soaked in warm milk
  • Fresh mint and coriander leaves

Instructions

  1. Marinate: Mix the chicken with yogurt, half the fried onions, ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, fresh herbs, and salt. Marinate for at least 1 hour (preferably overnight).
  2. Par-boil rice: Boil heavily salted water with whole spices. Add the soaked rice and cook until it is 70% done. Drain well and spread it out.
  3. Cook chicken initially: In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat some ghee. Add the marinated chicken and cook on medium heat until the chicken is slightly sealed and lightly cooked on the outside.
  4. Layering (Dum): Take the pot off the heat. Spread the par-boiled rice completely over the chicken. Drizzle with the saffron milk, ghee, leftover fried onions, and more chopped mint/coriander.
  5. Final Steam: Seal the pot tightly with dough or foil so no steam escapes. Cook on the lowest possible heat for about 20-30 minutes. Let it rest before gently completely mixing. Serve with raita.

Classic Chana Masala

⏱ Prep: Overnight | Cook: 40 mins 🔪 Easy

A highly popular North Indian dish of tender chickpeas cooked in a tangy, spicy, and earthy tomato gravy.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups whole chickpeas (Kabuli Chana), soaked overnight
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 tsp chana masala spice mix or garam masala
  • 1 tsp amchur (dry mango powder)
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tea bag (optional, for dark color)

Instructions

  1. Pressure cook chickpeas: Drain the soaked chickpeas. Cook them in a pressure cooker with fresh water, salt, and the tea bag until completely soft. Remove the tea bag.
  2. Base prep: Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds. Sauté the chopped onions until heavily browned, then add the ginger-garlic paste.
  3. Build the masala: Add the tomato puree along with turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and the chana masala spice. Cook until oil separates profusely from the sides of the pan.
  4. Combine: Add the boiled chickpeas along with some of the starchy cooking water into the masala base. Mix well and let it rapidly simmer for 15 minutes, allowing flavors to meld.
  5. Tangy finish: Stir in the dry mango powder for that signature tartness. Garnish heavily with fresh coriander and serve with bhatura or rice!

South Indian Masala Dosa

⏱ Prep: 24 hrs | Cook: 15 mins 🔪 Hard

A wafer-thin, savory crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter, wrapped around a spiced potato curry.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups short-grain rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • ½ tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 4 large potatoes, boiled and roughly mashed
  • 1 large onion, sliced thin
  • ½ tsp mustard seeds
  • 10-12 fresh curry leaves
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • Ghee or sesame oil for roasting

Instructions

  1. Batter soaking: Wash and soak the rice, dal, and fenugreek separately for 6-8 hours.
  2. Grind and ferment: Grind everything into a smooth, slightly thick batter. Mix thoroughly by hand (the heat of your hand aids fermentation). Leave to ferment overnight (8-12 hours) until aerated and doubled in volume.
  3. Potato Filling (Aloo Masala): Heat oil, crackle the mustard seeds and curry leaves. Add onions and green chilies. Sauté until soft. Add mashed potatoes, turmeric, and salt. Mix well and set aside.
  4. Spread the Dosa: Heat a flat griddle (tava) heavily. Wipe it cleanly with a damp cloth. Pour a ladle of fermented batter into the center and quickly spread it outwards in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.
  5. Roast and wrap: Drizzle ghee around the edges. Once the bottom is beautifully golden and crispy, place a scoop of the potato filling in the center, fold it in half, and serve hot with sambar and coconut chutney.

Gulab Jamun

⏱ Prep: 20 mins | Cook: 20 mins 🔪 Medium

Golden, deep-fried milk solid spheres soaked in an aromatic sugar syrup flavored with rose water and cardamom.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Khoya (dried milk solids) or milk powder
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • Warm milk (to knead)
  • 1.5 cups sugar
  • 1.5 cups water
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1 tsp rose water
  • Ghee or neutral oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Make sugar syrup: Boil water and sugar together until the sugar fully dissolves. Add crushed cardamom pods. Simmer for 5 minutes until somewhat sticky (but not stringy). Turn off heat and stir in the rose water.
  2. Prepare dough: In a wide bowl, gently mix the khoya (or milk powder), flour, and baking powder. Add tiny splashes of warm milk to bind everything into a soft, smooth dough. Do not over-knead.
  3. Roll the balls: Pinch off small portions of the dough and roll them into completely smooth, crack-free balls. Ghee on your palms helps with shaping.
  4. Frying: Heat plenty of ghee/oil on low-medium heat. The temperature is critical: it shouldn't be too hot. Add the balls; they should sink and gently rise. Fry until deep golden brown, stirring occasionally for even coloring.
  5. Soaking: Drain the hot jamuns and drop them directly into the warm (not hot nor cold) sugar syrup. Let them soak for at least 2 hours so they become incredibly soft, spongy, and steeped in syrup.
